Who We Are

Joshua Choi and Tristan Chan are two high school students who met at the Cité Scolaire Internationale de Lyon in Gerland, France. With a shared interest in economics and finance, they launched this platform to make complex financial topics more accessible to the public.

Their primary focus is on helping users better understand European Central Bank (ECB) interest rates—what drives them, how they evolve, and the broader impact they have on the European economy. By breaking down these concepts and offering clear, practical insights, Joshua and Tristan aim to equip individual borrowers and small business owners with the knowledge they need to make informed financial decisions and plan more effectively in a changing economic landscape.

Main Refinancing Rate - Historical Overview (1999-2025)

Main Refinancing Rate - 1999 to 2025

This rate represents the cost for banks to borrow money from the ECB. It influences general lending rates across the economy and is the primary tool for controlling inflation and economic growth.

Deposit Facility Rate - Historical Overview (1999-2025)

Deposit Facility Rate - 1999 to 2025

This rate determines the interest banks receive for depositing money overnight at the ECB. It sets the floor for short-term interest rates in the euro area.

Marginal Lending Rate - Historical Overview (1999-2025)

Marginal Lending Rate - 1999 to 2025

This is the interest rate banks pay when borrowing from the ECB overnight. It sets the ceiling for short-term interest rates and is typically higher than the main refinancing rate.
